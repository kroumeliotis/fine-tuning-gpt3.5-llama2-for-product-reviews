# LLMs in eCommerce: A Comparative Analysis of GPT and LLaMA Models in Product Review Evaluation

**Description**:  This opensource software provides support for the scientific documentation presented in the research article entitled "Fine-Tuning Large Language Models (LLM): A Comparative Study of GPT-3.5 and LLama 2 for Enhanced E-commerce Review Satisfaction Analysis" that has been published in "" academic journal.

**Aim**: The present research endeavors to comprehend the sentiment underlying each product review through the utilization of Large Language Models. The models employed in this study consist of GPT-3.5 and Llama-2. Initially, a Chrome application was developed to collect reviews from popular MarketPlaces and E-commerce platforms using JavaScript. Subsequently, a Python and Flask-based backend environment was established to receive requests from the Chrome application and store the data in a SQLite database. The collected data was divided into training and validation, and testing sets. In this study, our objective is to illustrate the effectivenesswe aim to demonstrate the efficiency of LLMs by conducting through a comparative analysis of two cutting-edge LLMs models, GPT- 3.5 and LLaMA-Llama 2, along with two additional NLP models, BERT and RoBERTa. We will evaluate the performance of these models before and after fine-tuning them specifically for product review sentiment analysis. The primary goal of this research is to determine if these specific LLMs, to what extent, can assist in understanding customer satisfaction within the context of an eCommerce environment. By comparing the effectiveness of these models, we seek to shed light on the potential impact of LLMs on customer satisfaction analysis and enhance our understanding of their capabilities in this context.
